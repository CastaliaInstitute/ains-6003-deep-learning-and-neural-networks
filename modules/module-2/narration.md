# Module 2 Narration

## Slide 1 Narration

Explain that search is one of the oldest ways AI researchers formalized intelligent behavior. An agent often succeeds not by knowing everything, but by exploring possibilities in a disciplined way.

## Slide 2 Narration

Walk students through the anatomy of a search problem. Emphasize that every part of the representation shapes what the system can do well.

## Slide 3 Narration

Contrast uninformed and informed strategies. Help students see that the question is not only whether a method finds a solution, but whether it finds one within real-world constraints.

## Slide 4 Narration

Describe heuristics as structured shortcuts. Make clear that heuristics are not a sign of weakness; they are part of what makes intelligence practical.

## Slide 5 Narration

Invite students to connect the idea of search to everyday decision-making, including institutional planning, recommendation, and resource allocation.
